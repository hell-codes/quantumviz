Quantized Energy Levels

Particles confined in a potential well can only exist at discrete energy levels. Use the buttons to absorb or emit photons and observe quantum transitions.

Quantum Transition Explanation

The electron is in the ground state (n = 0) by default — the lowest possible energy level of the quantum harmonic oscillator.

Energy is quantized — particles can only occupy specific discrete levels:

Eₙ = ℏω ( n + ½ )
Eₙ — energy of quantum state n
ℏ — reduced Planck constant (1.055 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s)
ω — angular frequency of oscillation
n — quantum number (0, 1, 2, 3, …)

The ½ is the zero-point energy — even at n=0, the particle has energy.

When the electron absorbs a photon, it jumps up one level. When it emits a photon, it drops down and releases light.

The colour of the photon depends on the energy gap between levels.
