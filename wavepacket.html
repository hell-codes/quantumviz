Wave Packet Evolution

A wave packet is a localized combination of many waves representing a quantum particle. Watch it spread due to dispersion — a direct consequence of the uncertainty principle.

Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle

Δx · Δp ≥ ℏ / 2
Δx — position uncertainty (spread of the packet)
Δp — momentum uncertainty (spread in k-space)
ℏ/2 — minimum possible product (≈ 5.27 × 10⁻³⁵ J·s)

A narrow packet (small Δx) requires a wide range of momenta (large Δp), so it spreads faster. Drag the σ slider to see this in action.

If the particle is highly localized (small Δx), its momentum uncertainty must be large — causing the wave packet to spread faster. Adjust σ to see this directly.

Physical Interpretation

• The wave peak is the most probable position.

• The filled envelope is the overall probability distribution.

• As time increases, the packet spreads — different k-components travel at slightly different velocities. This is quantum dispersion.

• Narrowing σ (smaller Δx) instantly widens the momentum-space peak (larger Δp), enforcing Heisenberg's principle in real time.
